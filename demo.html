Scenarios

Three situations we are built for

We are VitalBee. Below are three examples of how our platform supports you when time is short and decisions are hard. In each case we focus on the same thing: clear maps, ranked priorities, and evidence you can stand behind with your teams, your agency, and the public.

1 · Urban collapse 2 · Landslide corridor 3 · Industrial night / smoke

Scenario 1

Urban earthquake — multi-block rubble

You
You lead municipal SAR operations.
The scene
A dense city, hours after the main shock, with façades still falling.
What you face
Calls from everywhere, and dogs and crews cannot safely sweep the whole area fast enough.

How we support you

  • We help you answer “where first?” without guessing across pancaked blocks. Our platform flies an adaptive grid over your priority area and fuses RF, acoustic, and thermal on board—even when dust limits what you see on camera.
  • You get a live probability map so cell G7 stands out when signatures repeat, not when they are one-off noise from damaged utilities.
  • You receive clear evidence lines (repeatability, relative strength) so you can move dogs and shoring to G7 first and explain that choice inside the command post.

What this means for you: Less random search, more coverage with the same teams—because you work from a ranked list, not from noise.

In short

Unlike video-only flights, we give you one ranked map you can cite when agencies or the press ask why you prioritized one pile first.

Scenario 2

Landslide — long debris corridor, no road

You
You lead a provincial disaster task force in a mountain district.
The scene
Kilometres of debris tongue, the access road gone, weather closing in.
What you face
Foot teams need hours to reach the toe; helicopters cannot hover low safely along the whole path.

How we support you

  • We close the coverage gap with a long corridor pattern along the debris flow. Acoustic and RF contacts are tied to GPS in minutes, without your people laying ground sensors along kilometres by hand.
  • You see ranked chains along the flow—not random dots—so Waypoint B comes first when the data show a stable pattern where vehicles may have moved.
  • You can brief rope teams and aviation with B before A and C, using our confidence order instead of gut feel down the valley.

What this means for you: Fast coverage of the whole slide and a clear order of effort when every hour counts.

Visual — debris tongue & ranked waypoints

In short

We give you mobility that ground-only tools cannot match along a long corridor. If visibility drops, our signal layers still tell the story—coverage and ranking, not only video.

Scenario 3

Industrial incident — night, smoke, bad visibility

You
You coordinate plant safety and mutual-aid SAR.
The scene
Partial building collapse, smoke and steam, hotspots shifting.
What you face
Thermal blobs may be people—or pipes and tanks. CCTV is down.

How we support you

  • We address unclear thermal with signal-first fusion: acoustic and narrowband RF cues from phones or wearables, with thermal held lower until the plume clears.
  • We run a verification pass that loiters, checks consistency over time, and highlights Bay North-3 with evidence your command staff can read under stress.
  • Your entry teams receive a geo pin and confidence band on rugged tablets so crews can commit to the north side first instead of spreading effort across noise.

What this means for you: Decisions you can defend when one wrong door costs lives—not only a dot on a map.

Visual — sensors → VitalBee → teams

In short

In a plant emergency you need more than a black box. We put evidence next to the pin so you and mutual-aid command can agree fast.
